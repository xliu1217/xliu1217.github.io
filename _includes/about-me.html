About me

Greetings! I am Xinyi Liu, currently pursuing my graduate studies at the Cockrell Engineering School, University of Texas at Austin. I have the privilege of being co-advised by Prof. Peter Stone and Prof. Qianwen Wang. My core research revolves around interactive visualization/VR and human-centered AI.

In the summer of 2023, I undertook the role of a post-graduate HCI researcher at the Nil Gehlenborg Lab, Harvard Medical School. Additional details about me can be found on the lab's official website. I completed my undergraduate degrees in Computer Science & Economics at the University of Maryland in Spring 2022, where I was lucky to be advised by Prof. Zhicheng Liu and published a paper at IEEE VIS 2022.

Eager to collaborate? I've always believed in the beauty of "visualization as the marriage between science and art." This sentiment is deeply rooted in my drive to draw research inspirations from everyday life. My ultimate aspiration is for my research to yield tangible benefits, enhancing people's lives in meaningful ways. Currently, I'm diving deep into the realm of interactive visualization in virtual reality and nuances of explainable machine learning. Although my primary focus areas include healthcare, gaming, urban development, and food, I'm innately curious and always on the lookout to explore new horizons.
